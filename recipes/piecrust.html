Pie Crust

Description

Achieve the perfect pie with this versatile pie crust recipe. Flaky, buttery, and golden brown, this crust forms the ideal foundation for both sweet and savory fillings. With a simple blend of flour, butter, and a touch of salt, it creates a tender texture that complements any pie. Whether you're baking a classic apple pie, a rich quiche, or a savory pot pie, this crust ensures a delightful crunch and a deliciously crisp finish. Easy to prepare and handle, it's the essential base for your favorite pie creations.

Ingredients

  • 185g / 5.6 oz flour (plain / all-purpose flour)
  • 2 tsp white sugar (skip if making savory pie)
  • 0.5 tsp salt
  • 115 g unsalted butter, cold, cut into 1 cm / 1/3" cubes
  • 2.5 tbsp ice-cold water (+ more as required)

Steps

Make Dough:

  1. Pulse Dry: Place flour, sugar, and salt in a food processor. Pulse twice to combine.
  2. Cut in Butter: Scatter butter across the surface. Pulse 5 times until the largest pieces are the size of chickpeas.
  3. Add Chilled Water: With the motor running on low, pour 2.5 tbsp of water into the tube feeder.
  4. Form Crumbs: Keep blitzing for 10 seconds until crumbs form (also see video). Pinch between fingers - they should stick and form a dough.
  5. HAND Option: Whisk flour, sugar, and salt in a large bowl. Rub butter into flour with the tips of fingers until it resembles crumbs - it should look the same as using a food processor. Then mix in cold water with a rubber spatula, then proceed with steps below.
  6. Form Disc: Tip crumbs out onto a work surface, bring together into a dome (don't knead), pat into a 2cm/4/5" thick disc.
  7. Chill: Wrap in cling wrap then refrigerate for 1 hour (up to 2 days, otherwise freeze).

Rolling Out:

  1. Dust with Flour: Sprinkle the work surface with flour, unwrap dough and place on the flour. Sprinkle top with flour, dust rolling pin with flour.
  2. Roll Out: Roll out into a round that's 10cm/4" larger than a 22.5cm/9" pie tin. Patch up and roll over cracks as necessary.
  3. Transfer to Pie Dish: Gently roll the pastry so it wraps around the rolling pin. Unroll it over the pie dish. Drape pastry into pie tin (do not stretch/pull, causes shrinkage).
  4. Trim: If making a pie with NO LID (like Pumpkin Pie), trim edges with scissors leaving a 1 cm / 2/5" overhang. If making a pie with a lid (like a Meat Pie), trim edges so they align with the edge of the pie tin (i.e., no excess overhang).
  5. Tuck Excess Under: If applicable (i.e., per step above, if making a no-lid pie). Then crimp or decorate edge as desired.
  6. Refrigerate: Put pie crust in the freezer for 15-30 minutes (while oven heats up). This helps prevent shrinkage, firms up the butter again (flakiness!), and helps to ensure decorative crimped edges remain intact.
  7. Baking Options: (Note 4) - Blind bake the pie crust if it will be baked once filled (eg Meat Pie, Pumpkin Pie). Fully bake the pie crust if it will not be baked once filled.

Blind bake (par-bake, Note 4):

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C/390°F (180°C fan).
  2. Line & Weigh Down: Place 2 large pieces of parchment/baking paper crosswise over the pastry, then fill with baking beads or lots of rice or dried beans to weigh it down.
  3. Bake 1 Covered: Bake for 15 minutes, then remove from oven.
  4. Bake 2 Uncovered: Use excess paper to CAREFULLY remove hot beads, then return to the oven for 5 minutes or until the base is light golden (doesn't need to be 100% cooked though).
  5. Cool: Remove from oven. Cool 15 minutes in the pie tin before filling (another measure to avoid soggy base).

Fully Baked Option (Note 4):

  1. Follow directions per Blind Baking steps above BUT bake at 190°C/375°F (170°C fan) covered with baking beads for 25 minutes, then 15 minutes uncovered until golden. Fully cool before filling.

Recipe Notes:

  1. Cold Butter: The key to flaky, crumbly pie crusts is to end up with teeny tiny bits of cold butter throughout the pastry when it goes into the oven. The butter melts and creates air pockets, making the pastry flaky. Butter softens and melts as you work with it, which is why there are refrigeration steps and the recipe specifically calls for cold butter and cold water. If it's warm where you are, and it takes you longer than 5 - 7 minutes to roll out the dough, press into the tin, crimp edges, and get it into the oven, refrigerate the dough for 15 minutes or so before baking.
  2. Pie Weights: Pie weights stop the base from puffing up and help reduce pastry shrinkage.
  3. Baking the Pie Crust: If the pie will be filled and baked (e.g., Pumpkin Pie or Pecan Pie), follow the blind baking directions. The crust will be partially baked, enough to prevent the base from going soggy, and will finish baking with the filling. If the pie will be filled with something that will not be baked (like chocolate cream pie filling), then the crust needs to be fully baked.
  4. Different Measures in Different Countries: The measures in this recipe work with both US and metric (rest of world!) measures.
